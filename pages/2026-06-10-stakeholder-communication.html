利益相关者沟通 Stakeholder Communication

📅 2026年6月10日 · 星期三
A (Rebecca Torres) — Senior Project Manager, Apex Construction Group
B (David Okonkwo) — Community Relations Director, MetroCity Council

Dialogue

A:David, I wanted to loop you in early on our Riverside Quarter redevelopment. The planning application goes in next month, and I think it's crucial that we get the local community on board from the outset.
David，我想在Riverside Quarter重建项目的早期就让你参与进来。规划申请下个月递交，我觉得我们必须从一开始就获得当地社区的认同。
B:Rebecca, I'm glad you're thinking ahead. Riverside has a very vocal residents' association. If we don't manage the stakeholder communication with proper transparency, we could be looking at six months of objections and a PR headache.
Rebecca，很高兴你在提前思考。Riverside的居民协会非常活跃。如果我们不以恰当的透明度来妥善处理利益相关者的沟通，可能面临六个月的反对意见和一场公关噩梦。
A:Exactly what I'm trying to avoid. I'd like to propose that we hold a town hall meeting before the formal submission. Give people a chance to see the plans, ask questions, and feel heard. What do you think?
这正是我想避免的。我提议我们在正式提交前举办一次市民座谈会，让人们有机会看方案、提问，并感受到被倾听。你怎么看？
B:That's a sensible starting point, but I'd recommend something more structured — a series of focus groups with different stakeholder segments: residents, local businesses, and the school board. A one-off town hall can easily turn into a shouting match.
这是个合理的起点，但我建议更结构化的方式——针对不同利益相关者群体做一系列焦点小组：居民、当地商户和学校董事会。一次性的市民座谈会很容易变成吵架大会。
A:Fair enough. Focus groups it is. We've already commissioned an impact assessment covering traffic, noise, and green space preservation. The preliminary findings are quite positive — the new pedestrian zone alone would reduce rush-hour congestion by an estimated 18%.
有道理。就做焦点小组。我们已经委托了一份影响评估，涵盖交通、噪音和绿地保护。初步结果相当积极——光是新建的行人区就能减少高峰时段约18%的拥堵。
B:Those figures will help build buy-in, no question. But the last thing we want is for residents to feel they're being presented with a fait accompli. We need to frame this as a consultation, not a persuasion exercise.
这些数据无疑有助于建立认同。但我们最不想看到的就是居民觉得这是个既成事实。我们必须把这定位为征询意见，而非说服活动。
A:I take your point. Let me put it this way — what we need is a genuine feedback loop. We present the outline plans, collect concerns, feed them back into the design team, and then come back with visible adjustments. That shows we're listening.
我理解你的意思。让我这样说——我们需要的是一个真正的反馈机制。我们展示框架方案，收集顾虑，反馈给设计团队，然后带着可见的调整回来。这证明我们在倾听。
B:That's the way to do it. I can help you set up a dedicated project microsite with a Q&A section and a newsletter sign-up. Community outreach works best when it's multi-channel — digital, face-to-face, and print. Some of our older residents still prefer a printed newsletter in their letterbox.
就该这么做。我可以帮你建一个项目专题微型网站，有问答区和简讯订阅功能。社区外联多渠道效果最好——数字、面对面、纸质都有。我们有些年长居民还是更愿意在信箱里收到纸质简讯。
A:I hadn't thought of that. Alright, we'll produce a bilingual community update leaflet — English and Polish, given the local demographics. We'll issue a press release to the local paper at the same time as the microsite goes live.
这点我倒没想到。好，我们出一份双语社区更新宣传页——英语和波兰语，考虑当地的族裔构成。微型网站上线同时向当地报纸发新闻稿。
B:That certainly helps address the inclusivity concern. Now, on the timeline — I'd suggest a soft launch in Week 1, focus groups in Weeks 2-3, and then the formal planning submission at the end of Week 4. That gives you a full month of constructive dialogue before the paperwork lands.
这确实有助于解决包容性问题。现在关于时间线——我建议第一周软启动，第二到三周做焦点小组，第四周末正式递交规划申请。这样在文件落地前你有整整一个月的建设性对话。
A:Works for me. What about the council's own communications team? Should we coordinate messaging to ensure consistency?
我没问题。那市政厅自己的宣传团队呢？我们要不要协调口径确保信息一致？
B:Absolutely. I'll assign a dedicated comms officer to your project. One voice, one message — that's critical when you're dealing with a development of this scale. Any mixed signals and the rumour mill starts turning.
绝对要。我会指定一位专门的宣传官对接你的项目。一个声音、一个信息——处理这种体量的开发项目，这很关键。任何混淆信号都会让谣言满天飞。
A:Agreed. I'll need a sign-off from my board on the community engagement budget, but if we present this as a risk mitigation measure — the cost of doing proper stakeholder management versus the cost of a delayed project — I don't think I'll face much pushback.
同意。我需要董事会签批社区参与预算，但如果把这作为风险缓解措施来呈现——做利益相关者管理的成本对比项目延误的成本——我认为不会遇到什么阻力。
B:Smart framing. The business case writes itself — every month of planning delay from unresolved objections costs you roughly 2.5% of the total project margin. Compare that to a few grand on focus groups and leaflets.
聪明的表述。商业论证一目了然——未解决的反对意见每延误一个月，就耗费项目总利润的约2.5%。跟花几千块做焦点小组和宣传单比，差距显而易见。
A:Precisely. Let me draft a stakeholder communication plan this afternoon. I'll have it in your inbox by 6 PM. If we're aligned, we can present jointly to the residents' association committee next Thursday.
完全正确。我今天下午草拟一份利益相关者沟通计划，6点前发到你邮箱。如果我们达成一致，下周四可以向居民协会委员会做联合汇报。

Key Vocabulary

stakeholder — 利益相关者 | 任何受项目影响或有利益关联的个人或团体，BEC 听力和阅读超高频词
transparency — 透明度 | 公开、诚实的信息披露，利益相关者管理的核心价值观
town hall meeting — 市民座谈会/公开听证会 | 企业与社区公开沟通的常见形式
impact assessment — 影响评估 | 项目启动前对交通、环境、噪音等方面的专业评估报告
buy-in — 认同/支持 | 让利益相关者从内心支持项目，而非仅仅被动接受
feedback loop — 反馈机制 | 收集意见→反馈给团队→调整方案→再回应的闭环流程
community outreach — 社区外联 | 主动与社区建立联系和沟通的系列活动
constructive dialogue — 建设性对话 | 双方都致力于解决问题而进行的积极沟通
press release — 新闻稿 | 正式向媒体发布的声明，对外沟通的标准渠道
pushback — 反对/阻力 | 来自内部或外部的反驳意见，非正式但非常地道的商务用语

Sentence Patterns

It's crucial that we get... on board from the outset. → 我们从一开始就必须获得……的认同 | 项目启动阶段强调利益相关者参与的重要性
I'd like to propose that we... → 我提议我们…… | 提出建议的正式但友好的表达方式，比 "I suggest" 更商务化
The last thing we want is... → 我们最不想看到的就是…… | 用于强调应当避免的风险或负面结果
Let me put it this way — ... → 让我这样说——…… | 重新组织思路、换个方式解释的常用过渡语
What we need is a genuine... → 我们需要的是一个真正的…… | 强调核心需求的简洁有力句型
That certainly helps address... → 这确实有助于解决…… | 认可对方提议并点明其解决的具体问题

Learning Tip

利益相关者沟通（Stakeholder Communication）是BEC中级口语Part 2（Mini-presentation）和写作Part 2（Report/Proposal）的高频话题。考试中常见的命题角度包括：如何处理项目反对意见、如何组织社区沟通活动、如何向不同类型利益相关者传达信息。建议练习时关注"倾听→回应→调整"三步法的英文表达，同时记住"transparency"、"feedback loop"、"constructive dialogue"这三个概念经常被用作评分标准中的加分关键词。可以假想自己是一个项目经理，用英语起草一份两分钟的stakeholder engagement briefing，录音后自我评估流利度和术语使用准确度。
