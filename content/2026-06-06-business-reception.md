# BEC Daily | Business Reception

## Dialogue

**A (Rachel Wong — Business Development Manager, Pacific Ventures)**
**B (Alex Thornton — Regional Director, Bristol Partners UK)**

A: Alex, welcome to Shanghai! I hope you had a pleasant journey. How was the flight from London?

B: Thank you, Rachel. It's wonderful to be here at last. The flight was smooth — I managed to get some shut-eye, which is rare for a twelve-hour haul. I must say, the **hospitality** at the airport pickup was first-class. Your driver was already waiting with a name board.

A: I'm glad to hear that. We've put together a fairly packed **itinerary** for your three-day visit. Your hotel is about twenty minutes from here — the Peninsula, as you requested. We've arranged a **courtesy** car at your disposal for the duration of the stay, so you won't need to worry about transport.

B: Much appreciated. Before we dive into the schedule, I should mention that our CEO has asked me to **sit in** on a video call with the London office at 4 PM tomorrow. It shouldn't take more than forty minutes. I hope that doesn't throw a spanner in the works.

A: Not at all — I'll **factor that in** and shift tomorrow's factory tour a little later. We'll push it from 2 PM to 3 PM, which still gives us ample time. Speaking of which, would you like to **freshen up** at the hotel first, or shall we head straight to the office? The team is eager to meet you.

B: Let's head to the office directly — I can drop my bags at the hotel later. I'd rather make the most of the morning. I've brought the **presentation deck** on a USB as well as in the cloud, just to be safe.

A: Wise move — technology has a habit of failing at the worst possible moment. Now, a quick **heads-up**: we've invited a few key stakeholders from our Shanghai subsidiary for lunch at noon. It'll be at a local restaurant renowned for its Shanghainese cuisine — we thought you'd appreciate an authentic taste of the city.

B: That sounds delightful. I'm a keen foodie, so you've hit the right note. On that topic — are there any particular **protocol** or dining customs I should be aware of? I don't want to put a foot wrong on my first visit.

A: Not to worry — the setting is semi-formal, so no strict rules. The only thing worth mentioning: if someone toasts you, it's polite to return the gesture later in the meal. Oh, and your hosts will likely keep refilling your tea — it's a sign of **attentiveness**, not a hint to leave.

B: Duly noted. I'll brush up on my toast in Mandarin on the drive over. One more thing, Rachel — my flight back on Wednesday is at 6 PM. Would it be possible to schedule in a brief **handover** session before I head to the airport? I'd like to leave a clear record of the action points we agree on.

A: Absolutely. I've already pencilled that in for 2:30 PM on Wednesday. We'll do a thirty-minute **wrap-up** in the boardroom, then the car will take you to Pudong. Now, shall we get going? I'll **give you a tour** of the office on the way in.

B: Lead the way. And once again — thank you for the meticulous preparation. It makes all the difference when you're six thousand miles from home.

## Key Vocabulary

- **hospitality** — 接待/款待 | 商务接待场景核心词汇，涵盖接机、住宿、餐饮等综合接待服务
- **itinerary** — 行程安排 | 差旅和接待场景高频词，指详细的日程计划
- **courtesy** — 礼遇/免费提供的 | 商务接待中表示免费提供的服务（如 courtesy car 礼宾车）
- **freshen up** — 梳洗/休整 | 接待来访者时的体贴用语，比"rest"更正式
- **presentation deck** — 演示文稿 | 指整套幻灯片，比"PPT"或"slides"更商务化
- **heads-up** — 提前告知/预告 | 商务口语中表示提前通知重要信息
- **protocol** — 礼仪/规程 | 正式商务场合中应遵循的行为准则
- **attentiveness** — 周到/细致 | 描述服务或接待质量的正面词汇
- **handover** — 交接 | 项目或事务从一方到另一方的正式移交
- **wrap-up** — 总结/收尾 | 会议或行程结束时的总结讨论

## Sentence Patterns

- **I hope you had a pleasant journey.** → 希望您旅途愉快 | 商务接待的标准开场问候
- **We've arranged for...** → 我们已经安排好了…… | 接待方介绍为来访者所做的准备工作
- **I'll factor that in and shift...** → 我会把这点考虑进去，调整…… | 灵活应变行程的实用句型
- **Not to worry — ...** → 不用担心/没事——…… | 消除对方顾虑的英式商务礼貌表达
- **I've pencilled that in for...** → 我暂定安排在…… | 非正式但实用的预约表达

## Chinese Translation

A: Alex，欢迎来到上海！希望您旅途愉快。从伦敦飞过来怎么样？
B: 谢谢，Rachel。终于来到这里真是太棒了。航程很顺利——我还小睡了一会儿，这对十二小时的航程来说很难得。不得不说，机场接机的款待是一流的。贵方司机已举着名牌在等候了。
A: 很高兴听你这么说。我们为您三天的访问安排了相当紧凑的行程。酒店离这里约二十分钟——按照您的要求，安排的是半岛酒店。入住期间我们安排了一辆礼宾车供您使用，所以您不用担心交通问题。
B: 非常感谢。在深入讨论日程之前，我要提一下，我们CEO要求我明天下午4点参加与伦敦办公室的视频通话。应该不超过四十分钟。希望这不会打乱计划。
A: 完全不会——我会把这点考虑进去，把明天的工厂参观往后推一些。从下午2点调整到3点，时间仍然充裕。说到这个，您想先到酒店梳洗休整一下，还是我们直接去办公室？团队非常期待见到您。
B: 我们直接去办公室吧——行李稍后再放酒店。我更想充分利上午的时间。我带了演示文稿，既在U盘里也在云端，以防万一。
A: 明智之举——科技总在最糟的时候出问题。对了，提前告知一下：我们邀请了上海子公司的几位关键利益相关人中午一起午餐。地点在一家以本帮菜闻名的本地餐厅——我们想您会喜欢这座城市的正宗味道。
B: 听起来很美妙。我是个热衷美食的人，你们正中红心。说到这个——有没有什么特别的餐桌礼仪或习俗我需要注意的？我不想第一次来访就失礼。
A: 不用担心——场合是半正式的，没什么严格的规矩。唯一值得一提的是：如果有人向您敬酒，在餐席后面回敬是礼貌的。另外，您的东道主们可能会不断给您续茶——这是周到细致的表现，不是暗示您该走了。
B: 记下了。我会在车上临时抱佛脚练一下普通话的敬酒词。还有一件事，Rachel——我周三返程航班是下午6点。在去机场之前能否安排一个简短的交接会？我想把商定的行动要点做一个清晰的记录。
A: 当然可以。我已经暂定在周三下午2:30。我们在会议室做个30分钟的总结，然后安排车送您去浦东。现在，我们可以出发了吗？沿途我带您参观一下办公室。
B: 带路吧。再次感谢你们周密的准备。当你远离家乡六千英里时，这一切让人倍感温暖。

## Learning Tip

商务接待是BEC中级口语Part 2的常考话题。本对话涵盖了接待的三个核心环节：接机/抵达（journey问候、itinerary介绍）、行程中应对变化（factor in调整、heads-up预告）、送别前交接（handover、wrap-up）。练习时注意积累"体贴但不过度"的英式商务话术（Not to worry / Duly noted / Much appreciated），这在口语评分中属于加分的社会语言能力（sociolinguistic competence）。
