# BEC Daily | Merger & Acquisition

## Dialogue
**A (Diana Lam — VP of Corporate Development, NovaTech Solutions)**
**B (Richard Blake — CEO, Atlas AI, Inc.)**

A: Richard, thank you for hosting us. Our board has greenlit the exploratory phase, and I'd like to walk through the initial valuation framework before we engage the investment bankers.

B: Absolutely, Diana. Atlas AI has been bootstrapped for six years, so this is new territory for us. I want to be transparent — our founding team is emotionally invested, but we're pragmatic about the numbers.

A: That balance is exactly what makes a deal work. Based on your FY2025 revenue of €18.2 million and a 34% YoY growth rate, our preliminary valuation places Atlas AI in the €140–160 million range, using a discounted cash flow model.

B: I appreciate the rigour, but I'd push back gently on the DCF approach for a company growing at our pace. The comparables in the AI SaaS space have traded at a median 9.8x revenue multiple over the last four quarters. That would put us closer to €178 million.

A: Fair observation — and our comps analysis did flag that. However, two of those comparables have since corrected sharply post-acquisition. The board is mindful of overpaying. That said, we're open to an earn-out structure: €145 million upfront, with an additional €30 million contingent on hitting revenue targets over the next two fiscal years.

B: An earn-out makes sense conceptually — it aligns incentives. But the devil is in the detail. What targets are you proposing, and crucially, would the founding team retain operational autonomy during the earn-out period?

A: We're thinking €25 million in FY2026 and €34 million in FY2027. As for autonomy, NovaTech operates a decentralised model. You'd report to the group CFO, but Atlas AI would remain a separate legal entity with your team at the helm. We're acquiring capability, not dismantling it.

B: That's reassuring. My next concern is retention — we have 47 engineers whose institutional knowledge is irreplaceable. If we proceed, I'd want a retention pool of at least €5 million in restricted stock units, vesting over three years, ring-fenced from the main consideration.

A: We anticipated that. Our HR integration team has already modelled a retention package covering your top 40 employees. But ring-fencing it is a new ask — let me take that back to the compensation committee. If we structure it as a separate management incentive plan outside the headline price, would that address your concern?

B: That could work, as long as the vesting triggers are tied to continued employment rather than performance, so the team isn't penalised for integration turbulence. What's your timeline for a binding offer?

A: If today's discussion holds, we can issue a non-binding letter of intent by next Friday. Due diligence would run six to eight weeks, with a target signing date in early September. The key dependency is regulatory clearance — we'll need approval from both the EU Commission and the UK's CMA given the cross-border data implications.

B: Agreed. I'll brief the board this evening and we'll reconvene on Monday with our position on the earn-out metrics. Diana, this is the most constructive M&A conversation we've had, and I've sat through four of them this year.

A: That means a lot, Richard. Let's make this one stick.

## Key Vocabulary
- **greenlit** — 批准/开绿灯 | 项目或决策获得正式批准的商务用语
- **discounted cash flow (DCF)** — 贴现现金流 | 估值核心方法，BEC阅读常见
- **earn-out** — 盈利能力支付/对赌协议 | 并购中基于未来业绩的或有对价安排
- **comps (comparables)** — 可比公司 | 估值中用于参照的同行业公司
- **due diligence** — 尽职调查 | 并购前的全面审查程序，BEC高频词汇
- **letter of intent (LOI)** — 意向书 | 正式谈判前的非约束性文件
- **regulatory clearance** — 监管审批 | 并购交易需获得的政府批准
- **retention pool** — 留任激励池 | 并购后留住关键人才的股权或现金激励
- **restricted stock units (RSUs)** — 限制性股票单位 | 股权激励工具

## Sentence Patterns
- **Our board has greenlit the exploratory phase.** → 董事会已批准探索阶段 | 用于宣布项目获得高层批准
- **I'd push back gently on...** → 我想温和地提出不同意见 | 礼貌地表达异议的商务句式
- **The devil is in the detail.** → 细节决定成败 | 提醒对方关注条款细节的经典表达
- **That aligns incentives.** → 这使各方利益一致 | 用于说明某项安排的合理性
- **We're acquiring capability, not dismantling it.** → 我们收购的是能力，不是要拆散它 | 安抚被收购方团队的常用话术
- **Let me take that back to the compensation committee.** → 我把这个带回薪酬委员会讨论 | 表示需要内部进一步审批

## Chinese Translation
A: Richard，感谢你的接待。董事会已批准探索阶段，我想在引入投行之前先梳理一下初步估值框架。

B: 当然，Diana。Atlas AI六年来一直是自筹资金，所以这对我们来说是全新领域。我想坦诚地说——创始团队对此很有感情，但我们对数据持务实态度。

A: 这种平衡正是交易成功的关键。基于贵方2025财年1820万欧元的营收和34%的同比增长率，我们的初步估值将Atlas AI定在1.4亿至1.6亿欧元的区间，采用的是贴现现金流模型。

B: 我欣赏这种严谨，但对于我们这种增长速度的公司，我想温和地提出对DCF方法的不同意见。AI SaaS领域的可比公司在过去四个季度的收入倍数中位数为9.8倍。这将使我们的估值更接近1.78亿欧元。

A: 合理的观察——我们的可比分析确实也注意到了这一点。不过，其中两家可比公司在收购后都出现了大幅回调。董事会很在意避免过高溢价。话虽如此，我们愿意采用盈利能力支付结构：1.45亿欧元前期支付，外加3000万欧元，视未来两个财年的营收目标达成情况而定。

B: 盈利能力支付在概念上是合理的——它使各方利益一致。但细节决定成败。你们提议的目标是什么？关键的是，创始团队在盈利能力支付期间能否保持运营自主权？

A: 我们设想的2026财年是2500万欧元，2027财年是3400万欧元。至于自主权，NovaTech采用去中心化模式。你向集团CFO汇报，但Atlas AI将保持独立法人实体，由你的团队掌舵。我们收购的是能力，不是要拆散它。

B: 这让人放心。我的下一个关切是人才留任——我们有47名工程师，他们的机构知识不可替代。如果我们推进，我希望有一笔至少500万欧元的留任激励池，以限制性股票单位的形式，分三年归属，不计入主要交易对价。

A: 我们预见这点了。我们的人力整合团队已经模拟了一份覆盖你们前40名员工的留任方案。但将这笔资金单列出来是新提出的要求——让我把这个带回薪酬委员会讨论。如果我们将其作为一项独立的管理激励计划，不计入对外公布的价格，是否能解决你的顾虑？

B: 这样可以，只要归属条件是持续任职而非业绩表现，这样团队就不会因整合期的波动而受影响。你们出具有约束力要约的时间表是怎样的？

A: 如果今天的讨论达成一致，我们可以在下周五前出具一份非约束性意向书。尽职调查将进行六到八周，目标在九月初签署。关键依赖是监管审批——考虑到跨境数据的影响，我们需要同时获得欧盟委员会和英国CMA的批准。

B: 同意。我今晚向董事会汇报，下周一我们就盈利能力支付的指标重新讨论。Diana，这是我今年经历的四场并购谈判中最有建设性的一次。

A: 这对我意义重大，Richard。让我们把这笔交易做成。

## Learning Tip
并购谈判（M&A）是BEC高级阅读Part 5和听力Part 3的高频场景。建议重点掌握估值术语（DCF、comps、earn-out）和交易流程词汇（due diligence、LOI、regulatory clearance）。练习时注意"温和反驳"的商务话术——BEC考试中，用分歧推进合作的表达方式比直接说"no"更受青睐。此外，关注数字的听力辨识（百万、百分比），在考试中尤为关键。
