# BEC Daily | Supply Chain Management

**A (David Liu) — Supply Chain Director, AsiaTech Manufacturing**
**B (Rachel Kim) — Logistics Manager, GlobalFreight Solutions**

## Dialogue

A: Rachel, I'm glad we could connect on short notice. The situation at Port Rotterdam is getting worse — our latest shipment has been sitting there for eleven days now. What's your assessment?

B: Unfortunately, David, it's not looking promising. The **backlog** at Rotterdam has piled up to over 40 vessels. We're anticipating at least another five to seven days before your containers can clear customs.

A: Five to seven more days? That puts our production line at serious risk. Our **just-in-time** model means we keep only a three-day **inventory buffer**. Any longer and we'll face a complete line stoppage.

B: I understand the urgency. Let me propose a workaround — we can reroute the next three shipments through the Port of Antwerp. The **lead time** extends by roughly two days, but it avoids the Rotterdam **bottleneck** entirely. Your **freight forwarder** can handle the documentation switch.

A: Antwerp could work as a short-term fix, but what about the cost? Rerouting always comes with additional **surcharges**, and our quarterly logistics budget is already stretched thin.

B: You're right — there's a €1,850 rerouting fee per container. But weigh that against the cost of a production shutdown. I'd estimate the surcharge is roughly 3% of what you'd lose in a two-day stoppage. It's the lesser of two evils.

A: Fair analysis. Let's activate the Antwerp reroute for the next three shipments. While we're at it, I think we need to seriously discuss **supplier diversification**. Relying on a single European port route has proven far too vulnerable.

B: That's a strategic conversation worth having. I can put together a **risk assessment** report comparing alternative routes through Southern Europe and even direct rail from China. Would next Tuesday work for a deeper dive?

A: Next Tuesday at 10 AM works. In the meantime, please expedite the Antwerp paperwork. I'll brief our production team so they can adjust their scheduling accordingly.

B: Consider it done. I'll send you the revised shipping schedule and the reroute confirmation by end of business today. And David — these disruptions are likely to persist through Q3, so building a more resilient supply chain now will pay dividends later.

## Key Vocabulary
- **backlog** — 积压/待处理的工作量 | 供应链中断时的核心术语，BEC阅读高频词
- **just-in-time (JIT)** — 准时制生产 | 精益制造的核心概念，追求最小化库存
- **inventory buffer** — 库存缓冲/安全库存 | 应对供应链波动的基础策略
- **lead time** — 交货周期/前置时间 | 从下单到收货的总时长，供应链管理核心指标
- **bottleneck** — 瓶颈 | 限制整个流程效率的关键节点
- **freight forwarder** — 货运代理 | 国际物流中负责安排运输的关键中介
- **surcharge** — 附加费 | 运输中的额外费用，如燃油附加费、改道费等
- **supplier diversification** — 供应商多元化 | 供应链风险管理的核心策略
- **risk assessment** — 风险评估 | 商务决策前的标准分析和预判流程

## Sentence Patterns
- <code>I'm glad we could connect on short notice.</code> → 很高兴我们能临时约上 | 紧急情况下的高效开场白
- <code>What's your assessment?</code> → 你的评估是什么？ | 向专业人士征求判断的标准商务问法
- <code>...puts our production line at serious risk.</code> → ...让我们的生产线面临严重风险 | 表达紧急性和后果严重性
- <code>Weigh that against...</code> → 把这个与...权衡一下 | 引导对方做成本收益比较的经典表达
- <code>Consider it done.</code> → 放心吧，交给我了 | 表示立即执行、让对方放心的商务承诺
- <code>...will pay dividends later.</code> → ...将来会带来回报 | 用投资比喻强调长期战略价值

## Chinese Translation
A: Rachel，很高兴我们能临时约上通话。鹿特丹港的情况越来越糟——我们最新一批货已经在那里滞留了11天。你的评估是什么？

B: 很不幸，David，情况不容乐观。鹿特丹港的积压已经堆积了超过40艘船。我们预计你的集装箱至少还需要五到七天才能通关。

A: 还要五到七天？这让我们的生产线面临严重风险。我们的准时制生产模式只保留三天的库存缓冲。再拖下去，我们将面临全面停产。

B: 我理解这个紧急情况。让我提出一个变通方案——我们可以将接下来三批货物改道经安特卫普港。交货周期大约延长两天，但完全避开了鹿特丹港的瓶颈。你们的货运代理可以处理单证变更。

A: 安特卫普作为短期方案可能可行，但成本呢？改道总是伴随附加费，而我们季度的物流预算已经很紧张了。

B: 你说得对——每个集装箱有1850欧元的改道费。但要权衡一下停产的损失。我估计附加费大约只有两天停产损失的3%。两害相权取其轻。

A: 分析得有道理。那就启动接下来三批货的安特卫普改道方案。顺便说一下，我觉得我们需要认真讨论供应商多元化的问题。依赖单一的欧洲港口路线已经证明太脆弱了。

B: 这是一个值得深入探讨的战略话题。我可以准备一份风险评估报告，比较经南欧甚至中国直达铁路的替代路线。下周二做一次深入讨论怎么样？

A: 下周二上午10点可以。同时，请加快安特卫普的单证手续。我会向生产团队做简报，让他们相应调整排产。

B: 放心吧，交给我了。今天下班前我会发给你修订后的船期表和改道确认书。还有，David——这些中断很可能持续到第三季度，所以现在建立一个更具韧性的供应链，将来会带来回报。

## Learning Tip
供应链管理（Supply Chain Management）是BEC中级听力和阅读的常见主题。建议重点掌握 JIT、lead time、bottleneck 等高频术语，同时注意谈判中"问题→分析→替代方案→权衡成本→达成共识"的逻辑推进结构。多做供应链中断场景的角色扮演练习，对口语Part 3尤其有帮助。
