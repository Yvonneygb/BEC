# BEC Daily | Customer Service

**A (Emma Foster) — Customer Relations Manager, CloudNet Telecom**
**B (James Carter) — Operations Director, BrightPath Logistics**

## Dialogue

A: Good afternoon, Mr. Carter. I understand you've been experiencing connectivity issues. Let me pull up your account — you're on our Enterprise Plus plan with 12 branches, correct?

B: That's right, and I'm frankly at my wit's end. This is the third **outage** this month, Emma. Our warehouse management system went down for four hours yesterday. We lost thousands in unshipped orders. This is completely unacceptable.

A: I hear your frustration, and I want to be completely transparent. Our technical team has traced the **root cause** to a faulty firmware update on the regional server node serving your area. They've already rolled it back, but I understand that doesn't undo the damage.

B: Rolling back after the fact isn't enough. Our **service-level agreement** guarantees 99.9% uptime, and this month we're barely at 96%. I want to discuss what **compensation** you're prepared to offer, and I want a concrete **turnaround time** for a permanent fix.

A: Those are fair requests. Let me address them in order. On compensation — per our SLA, any month below 99.5% uptime triggers an automatic 15% service credit. I'm also authorised to offer a **goodwill gesture** of one month's service fees waived as a show of our commitment.

B: A month's waiver plus the SLA credit — that's a start. But my bigger concern is prevention. What guarantees can you give me that this won't happen again in two weeks?

A: Here's our action plan. We're deploying a **redundancy** system across all regional servers, which means if one node fails, your traffic automatically switches to a backup within 30 seconds. The rollout completes in ten business days. Your **account manager** will send you weekly status updates.

B: Ten business days. I'm going to hold you to that, Emma. And I'd like to **escalate** this formally — please put everything we've discussed in writing and copy your regional director.

A: Absolutely. I'll draft the **incident report** today with the compensation details, action plan, and timeline. I'm also scheduling a follow-up call for two weeks from now so we can review the redundancy deployment together. Does that work for you?

B: Yes, that works. I appreciate the proactive follow-up. We've been clients for five years, Emma — I want to keep it that way, but reliability is non-negotiable for our operations.

A: Completely understood, Mr. Carter. We value the partnership deeply and I'm committed to restoring your confidence. You'll have the incident report in your inbox within the hour.

## Key Vocabulary
- **outage** — 服务中断/宕机 | 技术服务场景的高频词，指系统无法使用
- **root cause** — 根本原因 | 问题分析的标准术语，强调找到源头而非表象
- **service-level agreement (SLA)** — 服务水平协议 | BEC阅读和听力中的核心合同概念
- **compensation** — 赔偿/补偿 | 客户投诉处理中的关键环节
- **turnaround time** — 响应/解决时间 | 服务承诺中的核心时间指标
- **goodwill gesture** — 善意表示 | 超出合同义务的补偿，体现客户关系维护诚意
- **redundancy** — 冗余（系统） | IT基础设施中防止单点故障的备份机制
- **escalate** — 升级/上报 | 将问题提交给更高层级处理的标准流程
- **incident report** — 事件报告 | 问题发生后的正式书面记录和分析
- **account manager** — 客户经理 | 商务关系中维护客户关系的关键对接人

## Sentence Patterns
- <code>I'm frankly at my wit's end.</code> → 我真心已经无计可施了 | 表达严重不满和疲惫的习语，比简单说 angry 更有力
- <code>I hear your frustration, and I want to be completely transparent.</code> → 我理解你的不满，我想完全坦诚 | 客户投诉处理的黄金开场——先共情，再透明
- <code>Those are fair requests. Let me address them in order.</code> → 这些要求合情合理，让我逐一回应 | 结构化回应客户多项诉求的经典句式
- <code>I'm going to hold you to that.</code> → 我会按这个标准来要求你 | 表达严肃期望，让对方知道你会追踪后续
- <code>...but my bigger concern is prevention.</code> → ...但我更大的担忧是预防 | 从赔偿转向根本解决方案的过渡句式
- <code>We value the partnership deeply.</code> → 我们非常珍视这段合作关系 | 安抚客户、表达长期合作意愿的标准表达

## Chinese Translation
A: 下午好，Carter先生。我了解到你们遇到了网络连接问题。让我调出你的账户——你是企业增强版套餐，有12个分点，对吗？

B: 没错，我真心已经无计可施了。Emma，这是这个月的第三次服务中断了。我们的仓库管理系统昨天宕机了四个小时。我们损失了数千件未发货的订单。这完全无法接受。

A: 我理解你的不满，我想完全坦诚。我们的技术团队已将根本原因追溯到一台影响你所在区域的区域服务器节点上的故障固件更新。他们已经回滚了，但我明白这无法挽回损失。

B: 事后回滚是不够的。我们的服务水平协议保证99.9%的在线率，而这个月我们勉强达到96%。我想讨论你们准备提供什么样的赔偿，以及永久性修复的具体解决时间。

A: 这些要求合情合理。让我逐一回应。关于赔偿——根据我们的SLA，任何一个月低于99.5%的在线率会自动触发15%的服务费抵扣。我也有权限提供免一个月服务费的善意表示，以体现我们对此事的重视。

B: 一个月免费加上SLA抵扣——这是一个开始。但我更大的担忧是预防。你们能给出什么保证，确保两周后不会再发生同样的事？

A: 这是我们的行动计划。我们正在所有区域服务器上部署冗余系统，这意味着如果一个节点故障，你的流量会在30秒内自动切换到备份。部署将在十个工作日内完成。你们的客户经理会每周发状态更新给你。

B: 十个工作日。Emma，我会按这个标准来要求你。另外我想正式升级此事——请把我们讨论的所有内容写下来，并抄送你所在区域的区域总监。

A: 绝对可以。我今天就起草事件报告，包含赔偿细节、行动方案和时间表。我还安排了两周后的跟进电话，届时我们可以一起审查冗余系统的部署情况。这样可以吗？

B: 好，可以。我欣赏你主动跟进的态度。我们已经是五年的客户了，Emma——我想继续保持这样的关系，但对我们运营而言，稳定性是不可妥协的。

A: 完全理解，Carter先生。我们非常珍视这段合作关系，我致力于恢复你的信心。事件报告一小时内会出现在你的收件箱里。

## Learning Tip
客户服务（Customer Service）场景在BEC口语Part 2和听力Part 2中频繁出现。投诉处理的核心模式是"共情（empathise）→ 解释原因（explain）→ 提出方案（propose）→ 跟进承诺（follow up）"。建议重点练习 SLA、compensation、goodwill gesture 等专业术语，并注意区分"投诉方"和"处理方"两套话术。
