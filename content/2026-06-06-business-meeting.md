# BEC Daily | Business Meeting

## Dialogue

**A (David Lin — Project Manager, Horizon Consulting)**
**B (Emma Frost — Marketing Director, NovaTech Solutions)**

A: Right, let's get down to business. Thanks everyone for making time. As you can see from the **agenda**, today we need to lock down the Q3 launch timeline and assign clear ownership of key **deliverables**.

B: Before we dive in, David — I'd like to flag that the creative team hasn't received the updated brand guidelines from your side. That's been a **sticking point** for two weeks now, and it's affecting our **milestone** progress.

A: Point taken, Emma. I'll **chase that up** with our design team this afternoon. For now, could we **table that** and focus on the launch **critical path**? We're already behind on the landing page wireframes.

B: Fine, but I want it on the record. Moving on — our team estimates we'll need six working days to finalise the campaign assets once the guidelines land. That pushes us to the 18th, which is cutting it close for a Q3 launch.

A: Understood. Let's **firm up** the following: wireframe approval by Friday the 12th, brand guidelines delivered no later than the 15th, and campaign assets by the 22nd. Does anyone have objections?

B: The 22nd is tight but doable. I'll need a **buffer day** though — call it the 23rd for final QA. And I'd like Martin from product to be **copied in** on all email threads going forward. There've been too many siloed conversations.

A: Agreed. I'll **loop Martin in** starting today. Now, regarding budget — Emma, your team's forecast shows a 15% overrun. Can you **walk us through** the numbers?

B: Sure. The overrun is largely driven by the **A/B testing** costs we didn't factor into the original estimate. We're running creative variants across three platforms, which adds about £4,200. I've attached the breakdown in the pre-read.

A: Thanks. Let's **greenlight** the additional spend but cap it at £4,500. I want weekly **check-ins** with the finance team to monitor this.

B: That works. I'll set up a recurring Thursday slot and send out the invite. One last thing — can we confirm the **go/no-go** criteria for the launch? I don't want us pushing live without a clear decision framework.

A: Good shout. The go/no-go hinges on three things: zero **P1 bugs** on the staging environment, full UAT sign-off from at least four beta clients, and the campaign assets approved and uploaded. We'll review status at the stand-up on the 25th.

B: Crystal clear. I'll update my **action items** by end of day. David, great meeting — this is the most productive one we've had in weeks.

A: Likewise, Emma. I'll circulate the **minutes** within the hour. Let's **wrap up** here. Thanks, everyone.

## Key Vocabulary

- **agenda** — 议程 | 会议前分发的议题清单，BEC 听力和阅读高频词
- **deliverables** — 交付物/可交付成果 | 项目管理核心术语，指项目必须产出的成果
- **sticking point** — 症结/卡点 | 谈判或协作中难以推进的关键障碍
- **milestone** — 里程碑/关键节点 | 项目管理中指重要的阶段性目标
- **critical path** — 关键路径 | 项目中最长的任务依赖链，决定总工期
- **firm up** — 敲定/确认 | 商务口语中表示将计划或安排最终确定下来
- **greenlight** — 批准/开绿灯 | 正式批准某个项目或预算
- **check-in** — 进度碰头会 | 短期、高频的同步会议，比"meeting"更轻松
- **go/no-go** — 通过/不通过决策 | 关键节点上决定项目是否继续的二元判断
- **action items** — 行动项/待办事项 | 会议中分配给具体人员的后续任务
- **wrap up** — 结束/收尾 | 商务场景中结束会议或对话的高频表达

## Sentence Patterns

- **Let's get down to business.** → 我们开始谈正事吧 | 会议开场白，标志非正式闲聊结束
- **Could we table that and focus on...?** → 能否先搁置这个，聚焦在……？ | 会议控场用语，用于引导讨论回到主线
- **I'd like to flag that...** → 我想提一下/指出…… | 用于温和地提出问题或隐患，比"problem"更委婉
- **Does anyone have objections?** → 有人反对吗？ | 正式会议中征求共识的标准问法
- **I'll circulate the minutes within the hour.** → 一小时内我会分发会议纪要 | 商务会议结束时的典型收尾表达

## Learning Tip

商务会议场景在BEC中级听力Part 2和口语Part 3中频繁出现。建议重点掌握会议各阶段的固定话术：开场（get down to business）、控场（could we table that）、征求意见（does anyone have objections）、收尾（wrap up / circulate minutes）。通过影子跟读法练习整段对话，同时关注转折词（however, regarding, one last thing）的逻辑信号，这些在考试中往往是信息点的提示。
