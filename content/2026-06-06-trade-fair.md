# BEC Daily | Trade Fair

**A (James Turner) — Export Manager, Heritage Craft Distillery**
**B (Sophie Laurent) — International Buyer, Le Marché Fine Foods**

## Dialogue

A: Welcome to the Heritage Craft booth! You've caught us at a good moment — I was just about to do a tasting of our new small-batch gin. Would you care to join?

B: I never say no to a gin tasting at 11 AM at a **trade fair**. I'm Sophie Laurent, International Buyer for Le Marché Fine Foods. We have 42 gourmet stores across France and Belgium. Your **booth** has been on my must-visit list — I've heard excellent things about your single malt.

A: Sophie, what a pleasure. I'm James Turner, Export Manager. The single malt has been our **flagship product**, but this gin is generating serious buzz. It's distilled with twelve botanicals, seven of which are foraged locally. Here — have a taste, and notice the juniper on the nose.

B: Hmm, that's remarkably smooth. The citrus finish is elegant — not too aggressive. I can see this performing very well in our **premium segment**. What's your **minimum order quantity** for international buyers?

A: For first-time partners, our MOQ is 600 bottles per SKU, with **distribution rights** typically granted on a country-exclusive basis for the first year. That means no other French retailer would carry Heritage products if we reach an agreement. But let me grab you our full **catalogue** — we also have a whisky cream liqueur that's been a hit in the UK market.

B: Country **exclusivity** is exactly what I'm looking for. The French market is competitive, and without exclusivity, I'm just another stockist. With it, I can build a proper brand story. What kind of **marketing support** do you offer to new distributors?

A: We provide a comprehensive launch package — branded tasting stands, point-of-sale materials, product training for your sales team, and a co-branded launch event budget of up to €5,000. We'd also feature Le Marché on our website and social channels as our exclusive French partner.

B: That's more support than I expected from a craft distillery. Usually the bigger players offer those packages. Can I take a few **product samples** back with me? I'd like my tasting panel to evaluate all three products before I submit a formal proposal.

A: Of course. I'll prepare a sample kit with the gin, single malt, and whisky cream — plus our detailed spec sheets and pricing tiers. Let me also give you my card. I'd suggest we schedule a **follow-up** call for two weeks from now, once your panel has had time to evaluate.

B: Two weeks is realistic. I'll be in London next month for another trade show — if the panel feedback is positive, perhaps we could visit your distillery then. It would be valuable to see your production process firsthand.

A: We'd love to host you at the distillery. It's where Heritage truly comes to life — seeing the copper stills and meeting our master distiller makes a world of difference in understanding what sets us apart. I'll send you the follow-up calendar invite today.

B: Sounds excellent. Enjoy the rest of the fair, James. I have a feeling this is the start of something very promising. Santé!

A: I think so too, Sophie. Let me get those samples ready for you right away. À la vôtre!

## Key Vocabulary
- **trade fair** — 贸易展会/博览会 | 行业展销活动的统称，BEC阅读和听力高频场景
- **booth** — 展位/展台 | 展会上展示产品的小型独立空间
- **flagship product** — 旗舰产品 | 公司最重要或最具代表性的核心产品
- **premium segment** — 高端细分市场 | 市场定位中的高价/高品质区间
- **minimum order quantity (MOQ)** — 最低起订量 | 国际贸易中供应商要求的最低采购数量
- **distribution rights** — 分销权 | 在特定区域或渠道销售产品的授权
- **catalogue** — 产品目录 | 系统展示产品线、规格和价格的资料
- **exclusivity** — 独家经营权 | 限制竞争、保障经销商利益的核心条款
- **marketing support** — 营销支持 | 供应商为分销商提供的推广资源和协助
- **product sample** — 产品样品 | 展会中让潜在客户亲身体验的小份产品
- **follow-up** — 后续跟进 | 展会或初次接触后的持续联系和推进

## Sentence Patterns
- <code>You've caught us at a good moment.</code> → 你来得正好/赶上了好时机 | 展会场景下友好、自然的开场招呼
- <code>...has been on my must-visit list.</code> → ...一直在我必访清单上 | 展会中表达高度兴趣和期待
- <code>I can see this performing very well in...</code> → 我能预见这在...市场会表现很好 | 买家表达积极商业意向的肯定句式
- <code>Country exclusivity is exactly what I'm looking for.</code> → 国家独家正是我想要的 | 买家明确表达核心谈判需求
- <code>I have a feeling this is the start of something very promising.</code> → 我有预感这是很有前景的开始 | 商务关系建立的积极收尾，表达乐观预期
- <code>Let me get those...ready for you right away.</code> → 我马上给你准备... | 展会上体现效率和诚意的行动承诺

## Chinese Translation
A: 欢迎来到Heritage Craft的展位！你来得正好——我正准备品鉴我们新款的小批量金酒。要不要一起试试？

B: 展会上午十一点品鉴金酒，我从不拒绝。我是Sophie Laurent，Le Marché Fine Foods的国际采购。我们在法国和比利时有42家精品食品店。你的展位一直在我的必访清单上——我听说过很多关于你们单一麦芽威士忌的好评。

A: Sophie，幸会。我是James Turner，出口经理。单一麦芽是我们的旗舰产品，但这款金酒正在引起不小的轰动。它用十二种植物蒸馏，其中七种是本地野生采摘的。来，尝尝看，注意鼻尖的杜松子香。

B: 嗯，口感非常顺滑。柑橘回甘很优雅——不会过于强烈。我能预见这在我们的高端市场会表现很好。你们对国际买家的最低起订量是多少？

A: 对首次合作的伙伴，我们的MOQ是每个SKU 600瓶，分销权通常按国家独家授予，首年有效。也就是说，如果我们达成合作，不会有其他法国零售商销售Heritage的产品。对了，让我给你拿我们的完整产品目录——我们还有一款威士忌奶油利口酒，在英国市场很受欢迎。

B: 国家独家正是我想要的。法国市场竞争激烈，没有独家权，我只是又一个普通经销商。有了独家权，我可以打造完整的品牌故事。你们为新经销商提供什么样的营销支持？

A: 我们提供全面的上市套餐——品牌品鉴展台、销售终端物料、为你们销售团队的产品培训，以及高达5000欧元的联合品牌发布活动预算。我们还会在我们的网站和社交媒体上将Le Marché列为我们的法国独家合作伙伴。

B: 这比我预期一家手工酒厂能提供的支持要多。通常只有大品牌才提供这类套餐。我可以带一些产品样品回去吗？我想让我的品鉴小组在提交正式方案前评估所有三款产品。

A: 当然。我会准备一个样品套装，包含金酒、单一麦芽和威士忌奶油——还有我们详细的产品规格表和价格档位。也请收下我的名片。我建议两周后安排一次跟进电话，届时你的品鉴小组应该已经有时间做出评估。

B: 两周很现实。下个月我会在伦敦参加另一个展会——如果品鉴小组的反馈是积极的，也许届时我们可以去参观你们的酿酒厂。亲眼看一看生产过程会很有价值。

A: 我们很乐意在酒厂接待你。那正是Heritage真正鲜活起来的地方——看到铜制蒸馏器，见到我们的首席酿酒师，对于理解我们的独特之处会有天壤之别。我今天就把跟进会议的日历邀请发给你。

B: 听起来太棒了。祝你展会接下来的时间顺利，James。我有预感，这是一个很有前景的开始。干杯！

A: 我也这么想，Sophie。我马上给你准备样品。干杯！

## Learning Tip
展会参展（Trade Fair）是BEC口语Part 2和听力Part 3的常见场景。展会对话的核心是"吸引注意→产品体验→商业条款磋商→约定后续跟进"。建议重点掌握 booth、MOQ、distribution rights 等展会和国际贸易术语，并注意区分展商（exhibitor）和买家（buyer）两套话术的差异——展商侧重推广和吸引，买家侧重评估和谈判。
